Design Summary: Realistic Rollover and Interbank Danger Zone
============================================================
Conversation summary (from "Realistic Rollover" through exit-restructure for bank j)

1. Realistic Rollover: Full P+I Repayment Every Period, New Contracts Every Period
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Core principle
  Maturity is NOT "interest-only while principal rolls silently."
  Each period:
    (1) Borrower repays principal + interest (P+I) in full via EN clearing (real cash flow).
    (2) Eligible counterparties may immediately sign NEW contracts (rollover).
    (3) RFQ / matcher still creates additional NEW contracts in the same period.

One-line definition
  Settle in full (P+I) -> immediately create new contract(s) (rollover + new RFQ trades).

Two conventions for rolling P+I into the next notional
  Convention A (conservative)
    - Payment: P × (1 + r) via EN clearing.
    - New principal: P' = P (same notional re-borrowed).
    - Interest is paid in cash and not capitalized.

  Convention B (more realistic for short-term interbank funding)
    - Payment: still P × (1 + r) via EN first (repay every period).
    - New principal: P' = P × (1 + r) (P+I rolled into next contract).
    - Borrower "repays in full, then immediately re-borrows a larger amount."

Where new contracts each period come from (two parallel channels)
  New contracts this period = rollover contracts (after maturity)
                            + RFQ / GNN match -> Trade -> Contract

  Rollover:     same lender-borrower pair often continues; notional ~ prior P+I.
  RFQ/matching: new counterparty or incremental volume.

Architecture alignment (ContractBook)
  - ContractBook: sole source of cross-period continuity.
  - exposure_matrix: snapshot re-aggregated each period from the book.
  - Do NOT rollover on the matrix itself.

Per-period timeline (normal operations)
  Start step t
    -> Pull due contracts from ContractBook
    -> Amount due = principal × (1 + rate)  [P+I together]
    -> EN clearing: full settlement
    -> If paid: remove old Contract
    -> If rollover eligible: new Contract (P', r', maturity = t + Δ)
    -> RFQ / matcher: new Trade -> new Contract
    -> Rebuild exposure_matrix from ContractBook


2. Danger Zone: Do Not End the Relation — Freeze Instead
--------------------------------------------------------

Motivation
  Approaching a tipping point should not immediately default and sever links.
  Instead: enter a danger zone, suspend flows, keep notes on ContractBook.

Zone model
  Normal
    Metrics OK. Full RFQ, rollover, EN settlement.

  Warning
    Drifting toward tipping point. Tighter limits; limited operation.

  Suspended (Danger)
    At / just before tipping point.
    ALL flows frozen: no new loans, no maturity payments, no rollover.
    Contracts remain on book with status = "suspended".

  Default (last resort)
    Hard breach after prolonged suspension without recovery.
    Resolution; relation may end.

Example tipping triggers (any one)
  - CAR <= 0.04
  - LCR <= 0.85
  - equity / liabilities <= 0.02

What "suspend all loans and returns" means
  For bank i in Suspended:
    - New RFQ / matcher trades: blocked (as lender or borrower).
    - Rollover: blocked.
    - Maturity EN clearing: deferred (maturity bumped; contract NOT removed).
    - Project investment: blocked (invest_free_cash, allocate_borrowed, update_project_book).
    - Exogenous liquidity shocks: paused for that bank.

  What is NOT done:
    - Contracts are NOT removed from ContractBook.
    - Counterparty edges remain (status = suspended).
    - exposure_matrix still reflects outstanding notional (frozen snapshot).
    - The relationship graph stays connected but inactive.

Exit criteria (resume)
  Hold suspension until ALL hold for M consecutive steps (e.g. M = 5):
    - CAR >= 0.06
    - LCR >= 1.00
    - equity / liabilities >= 0.04
    - Minimum suspend duration (e.g. 3 steps)

Hard default (optional last resort)
  If suspended > 30 steps AND CAR <= 0 -> is_active = False.


3. After Bank j Exits the Danger Zone: Handling Prior Borrowings
-----------------------------------------------------------------

Premise
  During suspension, debt was NOT erased — only cash flows were frozen.
  On exit, the question is how to RESTART settlement on frozen notes.

What exists at exit for bank j (borrower)
  - Suspended contracts: (l1->j), (l2->j), ... still on ContractBook.
  - Outstanding principal P1, P2, ...
  - Accrued interest: per policy (frozen or accrued during suspension).
  - liquid_assets: may reflect pre-suspension borrowings already received.
  - borrowed_cash[j]: should be 0 if cleared each period; if blocked, may need
    post-exit allocation to projects.

Important accounting point
  Previously borrowed cash already sits in liquid_assets.
  Exit handles the LIABILITY side (notes), not a re-transfer of cash.

Recommended four-step exit flow
  Step 1 — Inventory
    For each suspended contract (l -> j):
      outstanding_principal = P
      accrued_interest      = per suspension policy
      total_claim           = P + accrued (or P only if interest frozen)
    TotalDebt_j = sum of total_claim.

  Step 2 — Accrued interest policy during suspension
    Option A: Accrue during suspension -> heavier exit debt.
    Option B: Freeze during suspension -> exit debt ~ P only (forbearance-style).
    Recommended for forbearance narrative: Option B (or capped accrual).

  Step 3 — Restructure (recommended; NOT full immediate repayment)
    - Do NOT wipe debt on exit.
    - Do NOT require full repayment in the exit step.
    - Optional partial paydown: e.g. up to 10% of excess liquidity above buffer.
    - Remaining debt merged into one (or few) new ACTIVE Contract(s):
        principal' = remaining debt
        rate'      = base_rate + penalty_spread
        maturity'  = exit_step + 6 (example)
        old suspended contracts removed; RELATION preserved.

  Step 4 — Restart operations
    - j as lender: suspended lender-side notes return to active.
    - Re-enable RFQ, rollover, EN settlement under tighter monitoring until
      CAR/LCR stay above resume levels for several periods.

One-line rule for bank j
  After j exits the danger zone, treat prior borrowings as still-valid frozen notes:
  reconcile principal +/- accrued interest, then convert them into new active
  contract(s) with a longer maturity and (optionally) a small immediate payment —
  do not erase the debt and do not demand full repayment in one step.


4. End-to-End Logic Chain
-------------------------

  Normal period
    Each period: P+I settled via EN -> rollover new note + RFQ new note.

  Stress
    Metrics hit tipping point -> enter Suspended.
    Due contracts deferred; no new origination; notes frozen on ContractBook.

  Recovery
    Metrics stable for resume window -> exit Suspended.
    Frozen borrower notes restructured into new active contract(s).
    Optional partial paydown from excess liquidity.

  Return to normal
    P+I settlement + rollover + RFQ new contracts resume each period.

6. Design Evolution in One Sentence
-----------------------------------

From "real P+I settlement every period with same-period rollover and RFQ origination,"
to "under stress, preserve relations by freezing notes instead of severing them,"
to "on exit, restructure bank j's frozen debt into new active contracts rather than
wiping it or demanding one-shot full repayment" — a realistic, ContractBook-consistent
interbank continuity framework.
